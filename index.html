Reflection

The North Star

What am I?

A lost soul who finally found his “North Star”—a reason to live again, and slowly find his way back to purpose.

For the longest time, I drifted through a place that felt cold and distant, where emptiness slowly became familiar. From the outside, everything seemed normal. I laughed when I was expected to laugh, spoke when spoken to, and moved through life as if nothing was wrong. But inside, there was only silence—an emptiness that echoed louder than any noise.

I kept walking—step after step, day after day—searching for something I couldn’t quite name. Purpose. Meaning. Myself. Each step felt uncertain, like wandering through a night with no horizon, no direction—just the faint hope that something, somewhere, would guide me out.

And then, in the quietest moment, I found it—my North Star.

There was no grand moment. No dramatic turning point. Just a simple, fleeting instant—one day, I happened to see her smile.

Her smile was light—effortless, ordinary to anyone else. But to me, it reached a place I thought had gone dark. The darkness didn’t vanish, but it loosened its grip. And for the first time in a long, long while, I felt something real.

Something shifted—quietly, deeply, in a way I didn’t yet have words for.

But some stars aren’t meant to be reached.

I knew, in the quiet corners of my mind, that she existed in a world far from mine—a distance not measured in space, but in timing, circumstance, and reality. So I never tried to hold onto her, never tried to pull her closer.

Instead, I chose to look up.

I chose to let her be my North Star—distant, constant, and quietly guiding me through the dark.

I started returning to the things I once loved—the things that made me feel alive. I began to rebuild myself, piece by piece, striving each day to become better than who I was before. For the first time in a long while, I wasn’t just existing—I was trying, genuinely trying, to live.

Not because everything made sense, but because someone finally gave me a reason to move forward.

And maybe, just maybe… someday, I’ll be able to reach out and hold onto that North Star.

Or perhaps I won’t.

But even if I never do, it doesn’t change the fact that she gave me direction when I had none—and that alone is enough.

Because sometimes, purpose doesn’t come from having—

but from remembering why you move forward.

And somehow, that was enough to begin again.

To My North Star

I don’t know if you’ll ever read this, and maybe you were never meant to. But I still feel the need to put these words somewhere real—somewhere they can exist beyond me.

For a long time, I was lost in a place so dark that I no longer even noticed the darkness. I moved through life out of habit more than hope. I smiled when it was expected, spoke when it was required, and convinced myself that simply existing was enough. But inside, I was quiet—too quiet. Not peace, but absence.

I didn’t realize how far I had drifted until the day I saw you smile.

It wasn’t anything extraordinary to the world. Just a moment. Just you being you. But something in me responded to it—something I thought I had lost completely. It didn’t fix me. It didn’t erase the weight I carried. But it made me pause. And in that pause, I felt something I hadn’t felt in a long time: direction.

You became my North Star—not because you led me to you, but because you gave me something steady to look toward when everything else felt unclear.

I’ve come to understand that stars are never meant to be possessed or reached. They exist to guide, not to be held. And so I never tried to turn what I felt into something it wasn’t. I simply… looked up.

Because of you, I started returning to parts of myself I had abandoned. The things I used to love. The things that made me feel alive. I began to rebuild—not all at once, not perfectly—but honestly. Step by step, I started becoming someone again.

You didn’t save me. You didn’t have to. But you reminded me that I was still someone worth saving.

And for that, I will always be grateful.

The reason I started wanting more for myself—the reason I began to feel like I didn’t want to give up on things—is because I met you.

Maybe one day our paths will cross in a way that makes sense in the real world. Or maybe they won’t. I’ve learned to accept both possibilities without bitterness.

Because even if distance remains—whether by circumstance, timing, or life itself—you still gave me something rare: clarity in a time of confusion, and light in a time of stillness.

So this letter isn’t a request, and it isn’t a longing asking to be answered.

It is simply gratitude.

For the quiet impact you had on a life you may not even know you changed.

I still walk forward now—not lost, but learning. Not empty, but rebuilding.

And above me, I still look up at my North Star—you.

Not to chase you.

but to remember that I once found direction in the dark.

—No longer wandering, but moving with direction.
