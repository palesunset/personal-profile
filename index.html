About Me

The North Star

A lost soul who finally found his “North Star”—a reason to live again, a reason to rediscover purpose.

For the longest time, I was trapped at the bottom of a well—a place so cold, so dark, it slowly became my comfort zone. From the outside, everything seemed normal. I laughed when I was expected to laugh, spoke when spoken to, and moved through life as if nothing was wrong. But inside, there was only silence—an emptiness that echoed louder than any noise.

I kept walking—step after step, day after day—searching for something I couldn’t quite name. Purpose. Meaning. Myself. Each step felt uncertain, like wandering through a night with no horizon, no direction—just the faint hope that something, somewhere, would guide me out.

And then, in the quietest moment, I found it—my North Star.

There was no grand moment. No dramatic turning point. Just a simple, fleeting instant—one day, I happened to see her smile.

It was light. Effortless. Almost ordinary to anyone else. But to me, it was something more. It touched something deep inside me—something I didn’t even know was still there. In that brief moment, the darkness didn’t disappear, but it softened. For the first time in a long while, I felt… something.

And in that moment, something shifted—quietly, deeply, in a way I didn’t yet have words for.

But some stars aren’t meant to be reached.

I knew, in the quiet corners of my mind, that she existed in a world far from mine—a distance not measured in space, but in timing, circumstance, and reality. So I never tried to hold onto her, never tried to pull her closer.

Instead, I chose to look up.

I chose to let her be my North Star—distant, constant, and quietly guiding me through the dark.

I started returning to the things I once loved—the things that made me feel alive. I began to rebuild myself, piece by piece, striving each day to become better than who I was before. For the first time in a long while, I wasn’t just existing—I was trying, genuinely trying, to live.

Not because I had everything figured out, but because I finally had a reason to move forward.

And maybe, just maybe… someday, I’ll be able to reach out and hold onto that North Star.

Or perhaps I won’t.

But even if I never do, it doesn’t change the fact that she gave me direction when I had none—and that alone is enough.

Because sometimes, purpose doesn’t come from what we can have, but from what awakens us.

Now, I still walk—

—but no longer lost, and have purpose.

Label Switch Path Simulation Tool

I developed a simple simulation tool during my time in the telecommunications industry to validate traffic routing paths and simulate single-leg failure scenarios, helping to analyze basic network behavior and route reliability.

Network Capacity Simulation & Calculator

I developed a network simulation tool that models international and domestic traffic flows to analyze routing behavior, validate routing capacity, and predict outage scenarios. The tool also evaluates how traffic reroutes during failures and calculates the resulting capacity utilization on affected network segments.

IPAMS - IP Address Management System

A simple yet effective IP Address Management system developed to track, manage, and verify existing IP allocations, helping maintain network accuracy and prevent address conflicts.
